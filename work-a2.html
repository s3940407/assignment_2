Systems analyst

A systems analyst is a part of the overall it team, they are seen doing a variety of tasks ranging from working with their stakeholders and users to make upgrades to their software, hardware and overall IT system within the businesses. They also improve and monitor their systems making sure they work effectively in the business.

System analyst is a very big team player within the business.

System analysts need to have strong communication and problem-solving skills, so that they can properly convey the issues and solutions to the stakeholders and users making sure they’re able to comprehend it. Therefore, these analysts must have a strong understanding of the software and what the business will want if it is an improvement or implication. They also provide ongoing support and training for the other stakeholders in the business so that they can properly use the software implemented and if there is an issue or the software is not working how it was supposed to, they are to wok to fix things.

System analysts speak and interact with a range of people when working on projects and small tasks therefore having strong communication skills is extremely important. This is essential as the analysts need to properly convey the issues and solutions to the stakeholders and users, making sure they’re able to comprehend what is going and ways to resolve it. IT Professional #4 – Steve Ward:

Video #1

Networking is not all in front of a computer, network engineers gets to spend time in front of the computer doing designs but as well get to spend time selecting and purchasing hardware and time on site talking to the team that they support with their technologies, to be able to build something that is operational for companies as well as people on a day to day basis.

Network engineers get to design where to put wireless access points, and architect the design of the networking technology throughout buildings, institutions, to be able to support, voice , video and regular internet browsing.

Network engineers get to work with network switches which are like in the aggregation points that the wireless access points are talking to to be able to manage the whole architecture of the network.

Sometimes network engineers get to design what goes into the aggregation centres, from the cables to the servers and the switches that goes in.
